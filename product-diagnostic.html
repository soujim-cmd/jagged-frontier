The Product Health Diagnostic

Soujanya Madhurapantula  ·  May 2026

Product organizations that struggle to grow revenue usually have the same underlying problem: the product system is broken in more than one place, and the breaks compound each other.

A weak discovery process produces unclear requirements. Unclear requirements slow delivery and that delays customer adoption. Weak adoption produces shallow feedback and that makes the next roadmap worse. By the time leadership sees the anemic revenue growth, the root cause is buried in structural issues.

This diagnostic helps product leaders, engineering leaders, and PE operators find where the system is breaking and which lever to pull first. It connects directly to the operating toolkit and the GTM motion diagnostic. Fixing the product system without fixing the go-to-market motion only solves half the problem. And both depend on the foundation described in From Consumption to Outcomes: turning product usage into measurable business results is what makes the diagnostic actionable.

Product outcome → workflow cause → operational lever → revenue impact

Start where the pain is loudest. If you are not sure where the pain is, start at strategy and discovery.

Triage

Where to start

Six questions. Each points to a section. Any yes is a starting point. Multiple failures: start with strategy and discovery.

Strategy & Discovery
Are PMs building features customers rarely use, or struggling to explain why a priority is a priority?
Yes → start with Strategy
Build & Velocity
Is delivery slowing down, or are the same production issues surfacing repeatedly across installs?
Yes → start with Build
Portfolio & Investment
Are resources spread across too many initiatives with no clear prioritization logic?
Yes → start with Portfolio
Validation & Adoption
Are pilots not converting to production, or is adoption stalling after launch?
Yes → start with Validation
Feedback & Iteration
Is customer feedback landing in support tickets and CRM notes and going no further?
Yes → start with Feedback
Telemetry & Intelligence
Are decisions being made on instinct because usage data is not trusted or available?
Yes → start with Telemetry
Diagnostic sections

Six areas, same question sequence

For each section: what is the product outcome at risk? Where is the workflow breaking? What lever fixes it? What is the revenue impact?

Strategy and Discovery

Building the right things
  • Do PMs spend time in the customer's actual workflow, not just on calls?
  • Is the problem defined before requirements are written?
  • Is the roadmap tied to a specific business outcome, or is it a feature list?
  • Can every PM explain why their top priority matters now?
  • Are priorities changing from evidence or from pressure?
Revenue impact
Better discovery raises feature adoption on the same engineering spend. Fewer wasted builds. Faster time to value for new customers.

Build, Velocity and Tech Debt

Building things well
  • What share of engineering time goes to new features versus maintaining what exists?
  • Is tech debt tracked formally or discovered reactively in production?
  • Is cycle time improving or worsening quarter over quarter?
  • Do product and engineering share a clear definition of done?
  • Are the same classes of issues repeating across every new installation?
Revenue impact
More shipped value per engineer. Reduced incident cost. Tech debt addressed before it becomes the ceiling for scale.

Portfolio and Investment Decisions

Focus and capital allocation
  • Which initiatives would we stop if we had 20% less capacity?
  • Are there products alive only because nobody made the call to stop them?
  • Is the build versus buy versus partner decision made explicitly or by default?
  • Do we know the revenue and cost contribution of each product in the portfolio?
  • Is investment in new versus existing products intentional?
Revenue impact
Engineering capacity redirected from zombie products to high-value bets. Build versus buy decisions that do not create long-term liability.

Product-Market Fit and Validation

Pilot to production
  • Are pilots designed to mirror the real production environment, not a controlled setup?
  • Is success defined before the pilot starts?
  • Has the commercial conversation happened before the pilot ends?
  • Is adoption being tracked 6 months after launch?
  • Are performance, stability, and security blocking go-live after a successful pilot?
Revenue impact
Higher pilot-to-production conversion. Fewer stalled deals from foundation gaps. Faster commercial close after technical validation.

Customer Feedback and Iteration

Closing the loop
  • How does feedback actually get from the customer to the roadmap?
  • Do support tickets and CRM notes get triaged into action on a regular cadence?
  • Do PMs talk directly to customers, or does it go through CS?
  • Does every piece of feedback get a response, even if the answer is not yet or no?
  • Is win/loss analysis used to influence the roadmap?
Revenue impact
Customers who feel heard expand. Closed-loop feedback reduces churn risk. Faster iteration shortens the time between insight and improvement.

Telemetry, Competition and Intelligence

Seeing what is happening
  • Do we know which features customers actually use versus what they purchased?
  • Can basic usage questions be answered without a data request?
  • Can we identify accounts at risk before they tell us?
  • Is competitive intelligence commercial, not just feature comparisons?
  • Are north star metrics shared and acted on by the whole team?
Revenue impact
Usage data surfaces expansion opportunities before customers ask. At-risk detection before churn shows in billing. Commercial intelligence improves win rates.
From diagnostic to action

Choosing the right lever

Each section surfaces a problem. The problem can be addressed through process, people, tooling, or automation. AI apps are the right lever only when the workflow is deterministic enough to automate, the data is reliable and connected, and the organization is ready to trust and act on the output.

Process, people, or tooling fix

  • Discovery process: structure customer time into the PM workflow
  • Feedback chain: make closing the loop an organizational expectation
  • Portfolio discipline: set explicit kill criteria before initiatives start
  • Definition of done: align product and engineering before build begins
  • Automation and AI apps
When the problem is a process or a people issue, fix the process first. An AI app built on a broken product workflow produces broken outputs faster.

AI app candidate

  • Process and people
  • Discovery structure
  • Feedback chain
  • Portfolio discipline
  • Automation and AI apps
When the right lever is automation, apply the Go/No-Go filter before building. Business value, data quality gate, and org readiness all need to pass.
AI applications

Four apps across the six sections

These are the workflows where AI automation passes the Go/No-Go filter, assuming the data is connected. Each app addresses a specific workflow bottleneck identified by the diagnostic.

App Goal Data required Gate
Usage intelligence Telemetry Surface which features customers actually use, identify expansion signals, flag accounts at risk before churn appears in billing. Product telemetry, account data, usage and billing systems unified into a single view. Telemetry must be instrumented and trustworthy. Requires a unified data model across product and account systems.
Feedback triage assistant Feedback & Iteration Classify incoming feedback from support, CRM, and calls into workaround, backlog, or immediate fix. Ensure every item gets a response. Support tickets, CRM notes, call transcripts, product feedback submissions. Feedback must be captured in a consistent system. Requires clear triage criteria defined before automation is applied.
Portfolio prioritization model Portfolio & Investment Score initiatives by customer urgency, development complexity, and strategic value. Surface zombie products and the opportunity cost of inaction. Revenue and cost data per product, roadmap items, engineering capacity, customer adoption metrics. Revenue and cost contribution of each product must be known. Requires honest data, not advocacy from individual teams.
Competitive signal monitor Telemetry Track competitor pricing moves, packaging changes, and customer switching signals. Commercial intelligence beyond feature comparisons. Win/loss data, deal notes, public pricing signals, customer conversation transcripts. Requires defined signal sources and a process to act on findings. Only useful after internal telemetry is working.
