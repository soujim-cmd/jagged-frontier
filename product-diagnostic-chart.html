Product Health Diagnostic: Framework at a Glance

Soujanya Madhurapantula  ·  May 2026  ·  Read the full article

Product outcome → workflow cause → operational lever → revenue impact

The diagnostic at a glance
Revenue impact by section
Strategy & Discovery
Building the right things
Better discovery raises feature adoption on the same engineering spend. Fewer wasted builds. Faster time to value for new customers.
Build & Velocity
Engineering leverage
More shipped value per engineer. Reduced incident cost. Tech debt addressed before it becomes the ceiling for scale.
Portfolio & Investment
Capital efficiency
Engineering capacity redirected from zombie products to high-value bets. Build vs buy decisions that do not create long-term liability.
Validation & Adoption
Pilot conversion rate
Higher pilot-to-production conversion. Fewer stalled deals from foundation gaps. Faster commercial close after technical validation.
Feedback & Iteration
Retention and expansion
Customers who feel heard expand. Closed-loop feedback reduces churn risk. Faster iteration shortens the time between insight and improvement.
Telemetry & Intelligence
Installed base growth
Usage data surfaces expansion opportunities before customers ask. At-risk detection before churn shows in billing. Commercial intelligence improves win rates.
Four AI apps: goal, data, and gate
App Goal Data gate
Usage intelligenceTelemetry Surface which features customers actually use, identify expansion signals, flag accounts at risk before churn appears in billing. Telemetry must be instrumented and trusted. Requires unified view across product and account systems.
Feedback triage assistantFeedback & Iteration Classify incoming feedback from support, CRM, and calls. Ensure every item gets a response: workaround, backlog, or immediate fix. Feedback must be captured consistently. Triage criteria must be defined before automation is applied.
Portfolio prioritization modelPortfolio & Investment Score initiatives by urgency, complexity, and strategic value. Surface zombie products and the opportunity cost of inaction. Revenue and cost per product must be known. Requires honest data, not advocacy from individual teams.
Competitive signal monitorTelemetry Track competitor pricing moves, packaging changes, and switching signals. Commercial intelligence beyond feature comparisons. Requires defined signal sources and a process to act on findings. Only after internal telemetry is working.
